Recipe bank

Cooking is too complicated. Sometimes I'll be in the middle of making something and want to cry because I no longer want to do it and know it'll turn out tasteless anyway because the mummy blog I got the recipe off called for a teaspoon of salt and no more
So here are the things that are so easy even I can do them.

01. Chop a shitload of carrots and celery-- usually I do three of each
02. Then you chop an onion
03. Put heaps of olive oil in a big pot on medium heat and chuck in your veg
04. Stir them for like three minutes. When the onion's going translucent, put in
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 2 tsp ground fennel
  • Some saffron if you have it
  • I lied about the amounts!
  • Put in what feels right
05. Whatever you put in, now put in more. Once you've mixed it in and it smells really good, put in 2 tbsp tomato paste and a tin of tomatoes including their juice. Stir that
06. Drain a can of chickpeas, put that in. Any dried lentils in the house? Add a handful. Put in 750mL of veg stock
07. Boil, then simmer for 20 mins
08. While that's happening, chop up whatever green herbs you can find. Just go stupid with a big knife. Once the pot's finished, stir through that, minced garlic and minced ginger from jars. You're done. You will never ever taste a better soup
01. Chop half a head of Napa cabbage really fine (using the white bits is good)
02. Toss it in a bowl with 2 tsp of salt and let it sit on the bench for 15
03. Then chuck that in a clean teatowel, scrunch it like a wonton at the top, and squeeze the life outta it. Your sink should be full of cabbage juice
04. Chop your 3 fair-size spring onions fine and get them in a big bowl. Also your cabbage, which now looks like a really old Chux. Next, put in w/ them
  • 500g pork mince but it doesn't matter that much
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • Tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • Idk some soy sauce?
  • Mirin and sesame oil as a treat? Only if you have it though
  • Seriously just put in anything you think is more than this don't measure stuff
  • Chives might be good if you have them otherwise don't bother
05. Knead it all 2gether until homogenous
06. Get yourself a little bowl of water and a big plate and a packet or two of round dumpling wrappers which you can even get from Sarina Woolies in the fridge section so no one else should have problems sourcing them
07. Fold your dumplings and set them aside on your plate. If you can't do that, get good
08. Now, you can either boil them (fair, but low-reward) or fry them (proper, correct way)-- arrange them in rings inside your frypan with heaps of sesame oil over medium-high heat until they've got golden-brown bums, then put in half a cup of water and give them the lid. Once that's all evaporated, you have dumplings
